Update: can't get it to charge, when I have some time i'll try to bypass the battery management module and give a slight charge to the cells and see if that will wake them up, my main problem is that there is no connector for the battery so it is going to be a hassle to disconnect and reconnect it.

UPDATE!! if this happened to anyone else I was able to get the battery going by applying 3.7 volts to the plus and minus tabs near the battery. I charged the battery from there until the battery voltage was up to 3,6 volts and then I plugged in the USB C charger and now its charging fine. The battery itself has a BMS built in but it seems that Justine must has integrated some type of protection on the phone side also because otherwise I can't explain why it wouldn't have charged via USB C but connecting directly to the battery which has its own BMS yes.
